My academic hat

- the focus of my PhD

My PhD research has sought to understand whether we need to spend everyday stuck in an office. Is it possible for organisations to have teams that work from home and who still tackle complicated projects? My research has involved running an extensive experiment to compare the performance of local (co-located) and remote/virtual teams. I have been challenging teams to resolve a number of creativity activities requiring full team collaboration and problem solving.

My research question:

Are teams that work from home...

...as creative...

...as traditional, office-based teams?

(and therefore as good at identifying and resolving problems)

Not just work

- what I get up to in my downtime

Climbing

I take every opportunity I can to get out climbing in the local area. I love the freedom that traditional rock climbing provides. It enables you to explore the landscape in your own way, finding your own approach to a route and leaving without making a mark on the crag.

Cycling

I have been cycling since I was four and have never stopped. Some of my earliest memories are of watching Le Tour de France. I've stuck to watching the cycling, but still enjoy getting out and about Lancaster and the surrounding area on my trusty steed.

Music & Conducting

I have been playing the bassoon for over fifteen years. I'm very fortunate that my passion has seen me play in a wide range of ensembles, including tours of europe. More recently, I have started conducting alongside my playing. My next aim is to master the green trombone languishing at home.

Cooking

I love cooking and taking the time to find good ingredients. My family have always been foodies, with summers spent harvesting anything and everything on my parents allotment, to my Grandmother who re-branded Thursday as baking day. I tend not to navigate by landmarks, rather by bakeries, butchers, cafés, restaurants, pubs, bars and any other eatery.
